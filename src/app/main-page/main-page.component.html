A Brief History

2001

Elon Musk conceptualizes "Mars Oasis", a project to land a miniature experimental greenhouse containing seeds with dehydrated gel on Mars to grow plants on Martian soil, "so this would be the furthest that life's ever traveled"[6] in an attempt to regain public interest in space exploration and increase the budget of NASA.

2006
24 Mar

Falcon 1 demo flight 1 is launched from Kwajalein site in Omelek Island.

2008
28 Sep

SpaceX achieves the first privately funded liquid-fueled rocket to reach orbit (Falcon 1 Flight 4).

2011

SpaceX achieves the first privately funded liquid-fueled rocket to reach orbit (Falcon 1 Flight 4).

2012
22 May

Dragon reaches the International Space Station. SpaceX becomes the first private company to send a spacecraft to the ISS.

2015
22 Dec

SpaceX achieves the first landing of an orbital rocket's first stage on land (Falcon 9 Flight 20).

2017
30 Mar

SpaceX reuses a rocket for the first time ever. The Falcon 9 launches from NASA’s Kennedy Space Center in Florida with the SES-10 communications satellite.

2018

SpaceX begins launching Falcon Heavy, the world’s most powerful operational rocket by a factor of two.

2019
03 Apr

SpaceX’s next-generation spacecraft, a Starship orbital prototype, is planned to be used after mid-year for high-altitude, high-velocity testing.
